# Quantfore AI Product Positioning

## 1. One-line definition

**Quantfore AI is a thesis-change and risk/reward decisioning platform for investors — not an AI stock picker.**

It helps investors understand when the investment case for a company, sector, ETF, or portfolio has changed, whether the forward risk/reward remains attractive, and what action deserves consideration next.

Quantfore AI does **not** claim to predict the future with certainty, produce guaranteed buy/sell calls, or replace investor judgement. It exists to make investment decision-making more systematic, evidence-based, and responsive to changing conditions.

---

## 2. Category

Quantfore AI should define itself in a new but understandable category:

> **AI thesis-change and risk/reward decisioning platform**

This is intentionally different from:

- AI stock picker
- trading bot
- robo-adviser
- financial news summariser
- generic investment dashboard
- retail stock screener
- autonomous hedge fund for individuals

The category matters because “AI stock picker” sounds speculative, commoditised, legally risky, and hard to trust. “Thesis-change and risk/reward decisioning platform” sounds more institutional, evidence-led, and decision-support oriented.

---

## 3. Positioning statement

For serious self-directed investors, analysts, advisers, and investment teams who need to know when the facts behind an investment thesis are changing, Quantfore AI provides an AI-powered decisioning layer that tracks company fundamentals, market regime, valuation, catalysts, risk, sentiment, and portfolio exposure to identify changes in forward risk/reward.

Unlike stock screeners, generic AI finance chatbots, or black-box buy/sell signal tools, Quantfore AI builds a persistent memory of each investment thesis, measures thesis drift over time, and explains what changed, why it matters, and how it affects the risk/reward decision.

---

## 4. Core product belief

Most investment mistakes do not happen because investors lack information.

They happen because investors fail to detect, interpret, or act on **change**:

- the thesis deteriorates, but the investor remains anchored;
- the valuation expands, but expected return compresses;
- the company beats earnings, but guidance quality weakens;
- macro conditions change, but the portfolio remains exposed to the old regime;
- risk rises quietly before price action confirms it;
- the original reason for buying no longer applies.

Quantfore AI is built around the belief that the most valuable investment intelligence is not “what stock should I buy?” but:

> **“Has the thesis changed, and is the forward risk/reward still attractive?”**

---

## 5. Product thesis

The product thesis is that investors will pay for a system that continuously answers five decision-critical questions:

1. **What changed?**
   New filings, earnings commentary, guidance, estimates, margins, valuation, macro regime, sentiment, liquidity, price behaviour, and portfolio exposure.

2. **Does it matter?**
   Whether the change is noise, confirmation, deterioration, or a material thesis shift.

3. **How does it affect risk/reward?**
   Whether expected upside, downside, conviction, volatility, drawdown risk, or opportunity cost has changed.

4. **What decision does it imply?**
   Add, hold, trim, avoid, review, hedge, rotate, or wait.

5. **What would change the view again?**
   The specific future evidence that would strengthen, weaken, or invalidate the thesis.

## 6. Target users

### Primary user

**Serious self-directed investors** managing meaningful personal capital and making active allocation decisions.

Typical traits:

- invests in individual equities, ETFs, or thematic baskets;
- reads earnings/news but cannot process everything consistently;
- wants better decision support, not basic education;
- cares about risk-adjusted returns, not hype;
- wants explainability before trusting AI output;
- is willing to pay for workflow advantage.

### Secondary users

- finance creators and newsletter writers;
- investment clubs;
- family-office analysts;
- boutique wealth advisers;
- small fund managers;
- independent research analysts.

### Non-target users, at least initially

- day traders;
- high-frequency traders;
- options scalpers;
- passive-only investors;
- users seeking guaranteed stock tips;
- users wanting an app to trade automatically on their behalf.

## 7. Primary job-to-be-done

When an investor owns, watches, or researches an asset, they need to know:

> **“Has anything changed that should alter my decision?”**

Quantfore AI should become the system of record for that question.

The product should reduce three investor failure modes:

1. **Anchoring** — holding onto an old thesis after the facts have changed.
2. **Noise reaction** — overreacting to news that does not materially affect forward value.
3. **Risk blindness** — ignoring portfolio concentration, valuation compression, regime change, or downside asymmetry.

---

## 8. Core user promise

Quantfore AI helps investors:

- detect material thesis changes earlier;
- separate signal from noise;
- understand forward risk/reward more clearly;
- make better watchlist, portfolio, and allocation decisions;
- avoid stale theses and emotionally driven trades;
- document why a position is held, trimmed, added to, or exited.

The promise should be framed as **better decision quality**, not guaranteed investment performance.

## 14. Product wedge

The wedge is not generic AI research. Many products can summarise filings, news, earnings calls, and financial data.

Quantfore AI’s wedge should be:

> **Persistent thesis memory plus thesis-change detection.**

Most tools answer: “What is happening now?”

Quantfore AI should answer:

> **“What changed relative to the reason this asset was attractive in the first place?”**

That is the strategic difference.

## 15. Proprietary concepts

### 15.1 Thesis Memory Graph

A structured record of the investment case for each asset.

It stores:

- original thesis;
- key drivers;
- expected catalysts;
- risk factors;
- valuation assumptions;
- margin assumptions;
- growth assumptions;
- macro dependencies;
- sector dependencies;
- management claims;
- KPIs to monitor;
- evidence that supports or weakens the thesis.

The graph should update as new data arrives.

### 15.2 Thesis Drift Index

A score measuring how much the current evidence has moved away from the stored thesis.

Possible drift categories:

- no meaningful change;
- thesis strengthened;
- thesis weakened;
- thesis contradicted;
- valuation risk increased;
- macro sensitivity increased;
- catalyst delayed;
- downside risk increased;
- evidence quality deteriorated.

### 15.3 Risk/Reward Decision Score

A composite view of:

- expected upside;
- downside risk;
- valuation attractiveness;
- quality of fundamentals;
- earnings revision direction;
- price/volume behaviour;
- macro regime fit;
- portfolio concentration;
- liquidity and volatility;
- confidence in the thesis.

This score should not be presented as a magical prediction. It should be presented as a decision-support metric.

### 15.4 Prediction Ledger

A record of what the model believed at a point in time and what happened afterwards.

It should track:

- timestamped model view;
- input evidence;
- predicted risk/reward;
- confidence level;
- subsequent return;
- subsequent drawdown;
- whether the thesis changed;
- whether the model was early, late, right, wrong, or inconclusive.

This makes model performance auditable.

## 16. Competitive positioning

### Against stock screeners

Stock screeners help users find assets that meet current criteria. Quantfore AI helps users understand whether the investment case is improving, deteriorating, or becoming riskier.

### Against AI finance chatbots

Finance chatbots answer questions. Quantfore AI tracks persistent theses, monitors change, and structures decision evidence over time.

### Against quant rating platforms

Quant rating platforms often produce scores. Quantfore AI explains the thesis-level change behind the score, links it to risk/reward, and records whether the model’s prior views were useful.

### Against robo-advisers

Robo-advisers allocate portfolios based on user profiles. Quantfore AI is a research and decisioning layer for investors who still want to make their own decisions.

### Against trading bots

Trading bots automate execution. Quantfore AI improves investment judgement before execution.

## 17. MVP positioning

The MVP should be positioned narrowly:

> **A thesis-change monitor for US large-cap equities and ETFs.**

Initial universe:

- S&P 500 equities;
- Nasdaq 100 equities;
- major sector ETFs;
- major factor ETFs.

Initial horizon:

- 3 to 12 months.

Initial user workflow:

1. User adds assets to a watchlist.
2. Quantfore AI creates or imports a thesis for each asset.
3. The system monitors evidence changes.
4. The user receives thesis-change alerts.
5. The dashboard shows risk/reward movement and decision prompts.
6. The prediction ledger records whether signals were useful.

Do not launch MVP as “AI tells you what to buy.” Launch as:

> **“Never miss when the thesis changes.”**

## 18. Product surfaces

### 18.1 Asset page

The asset page should answer:

- What is the current thesis?
- What has changed recently?
- Has the thesis strengthened or weakened?
- What is the current risk/reward view?
- What are the top supporting and opposing signals?
- What should be watched next?

### 18.2 Watchlist page

The watchlist should prioritise assets by:

- largest thesis change;
- highest risk/reward improvement;
- highest deterioration risk;
- upcoming catalysts;
- valuation compression/expansion;
- macro sensitivity shift.

### 18.3 Portfolio page

The portfolio page should show:

- concentration risk;
- sector exposure;
- factor exposure;
- regime vulnerability;
- thesis quality by holding;
- positions with deteriorating thesis quality;
- positions with improving risk/reward.

### 18.4 Decision log

Every user or model decision should be recordable:

- added to watchlist;
- opened position;
- added to position;
- trimmed;
- exited;
- ignored alert;
- changed thesis;
- changed risk tolerance.

The long-term product advantage comes from learning which signals actually changed user decisions and outcomes.

---

## 19. Proof standard

Quantfore AI should not claim investment edge until it can show evidence.

Minimum evidence required:

- point-in-time backtests;
- survivorship-bias-free universe;
- transaction cost assumptions;
- walk-forward validation;
- out-of-sample performance;
- benchmark comparison;
- signal decay analysis;
- drawdown analysis;
- turnover analysis;
- model audit logs;
- paper-trading record.

The positioning should therefore avoid claiming “we beat the market” until the product has evidence.

Early claim:

> “Quantfore AI helps investors detect and understand thesis change.”

Later claim, only if proven:

> “Quantfore AI’s thesis-change signals have historically identified improving and deteriorating risk/reward setups before they were fully reflected in price.”

## 20. Pricing implication

The positioning supports a premium research workflow product, not a low-trust tip product.

Suggested pricing frame:

- Free: limited watchlist and delayed thesis-change alerts.
- Individual Pro: deeper thesis tracking, risk/reward scoring, and portfolio views.
- Advanced/Analyst: full model ledger, backtests, exports, custom alerts, and strategy views.
- Adviser/Team: client reporting, audit logs, shared research workspace, compliance features.

The strongest willingness to pay is likely from users who manage meaningful capital and value decision quality, not casual users looking for free stock tips.

---

## 21. Compliance posture

Quantfore AI should initially operate as a research and decision-support product.

The product should:

- avoid personalised financial advice language unless properly authorised;
- clearly explain uncertainty and limitations;
- show evidence and assumptions;
- avoid execution automation at launch;
- log model outputs and user decisions;
- provide educational and analytical framing;
- distinguish model signals from investment instructions.

The compliance posture should shape the product from the beginning, not be added later.

---

## 22. Internal product principle

Every product decision should pass this test:

> **Does this help the investor understand whether the thesis or risk/reward has changed?**

If not, it is probably secondary.

This means the product should prioritise:

- change detection over static dashboards;
- evidence quality over content volume;
- risk/reward over price targets;
- thesis monitoring over generic news feeds;
- decision logs over one-off answers;
- model accountability over black-box confidence.

---

## 23. External elevator pitch

Quantfore AI is a thesis-change and risk/reward decisioning platform for investors. It tracks the evidence behind an investment thesis — fundamentals, valuation, earnings, sentiment, macro regime, price behaviour, and portfolio exposure — and alerts users when the facts change enough to reconsider the decision. Instead of acting like an AI stock picker, Quantfore AI helps investors understand whether a position should be reviewed, added to, held, trimmed, or avoided based on changing risk/reward.

Quantfore AI should not compete as another AI stock picker. That category is crowded, low-trust, and difficult to defend.
