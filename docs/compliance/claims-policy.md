# Quantfore AI Claims Policy

**Version:** v0.1
**Date:** 2026-06-24
**Owner:** Product / Compliance / Founder
**Status:** Working policy

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines what Quantfore AI is allowed to say before the product has independently validated performance evidence.

Quantfore AI must be positioned as:

> **A thesis-change and risk/reward decisioning platform for investors — not an AI stock picker.**

The purpose of this policy is to stop the product, marketing site, dashboard, sales material, investor deck, model output, social content, and demos from making premature claims of alpha, prediction, suitability, or investment advice.

Until proof exists, Quantfore AI must sell **research support, evidence-backed monitoring, and structured investment decisioning** — not guaranteed returns, market-beating picks, or personalised financial advice.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Quantfore AI can claim to help users **analyse, monitor, compare, and structure investment decisions**.

Quantfore AI cannot claim to **predict the future, beat the market, know what users should buy, or provide personalised financial advice** unless and until the company has the required evidence, controls, regulatory permissions, and compliance approval.

The product can say:

> “This company’s thesis appears to have deteriorated based on the evidence we monitor.”

The product should not say:

> “Sell this stock now.”

The product can say:

> “This asset currently screens as having a more attractive risk/reward profile than its sector peers under our research model.”

The product should not say:

> “This is the best stock to buy.”

---

## 3. Regulatory Framing

This is a product policy, not a legal opinion. Legal counsel should review this before launch.

The working assumption is that Quantfore AI should initially operate as a **research and decision-support product**, not as an authorised investment adviser, discretionary manager, robo-adviser, broker, or execution platform.

The policy is designed around three conservative principles:

1. Marketing and product claims must be **fair, clear, and not misleading**.
2. Risk must be communicated alongside potential benefits.
3. Performance, hypothetical performance, model output, and backtests must not be presented in a way that implies guaranteed or personalised outcomes.

For the UK market, the FCA expects financial promotions to be fair, clear and not misleading, and automated investment services must meet the same regulatory standards as traditional advisory or discretionary services where they cross into advice or discretion.
For the US market, SEC marketing rules create specific constraints around performance presentation, hypothetical performance, testimonials, and misleading advertising by investment advisers.

Quantfore AI should assume that vague disclaimers are not enough. The product itself must avoid advice-like wording, misleading certainty, and unsupported performance implications.

## 5. Claims Maturity Levels

All claims must belong to one of four maturity levels.

### Level 0 — Concept / Pre-validation

Status: no validated model evidence yet.

Allowed claims:

- “Research support”
- “Evidence-backed monitoring”
- “Thesis-change detection”
- “Risk/reward framework”
- “Structured investment analysis”
- “Designed to help investors evaluate opportunities”
- “Built to surface changes in company fundamentals, valuation, sentiment, and risk”

Not allowed:

- “Outperforms the market”
- “Generates alpha”
- “Predicts stock returns”
- “Tells you what to buy or sell”
- “Beats analysts”
- “Proven model”
- “Institutional-grade alpha”
- “Best stocks to buy now”

Default public disclaimer:

> Quantfore AI provides research support and evidence-backed monitoring. It does not provide personalised financial advice, guarantee returns, or tell users what they should buy or sell.

### Level 1 — Internal Backtest Complete

Status: internal point-in-time backtest has been completed but not externally reviewed.

Allowed claims:

- “In internal testing, this signal showed evidence of predictive value.”
- “Backtesting suggests the signal may be useful for ranking research candidates.”
- “The model is being evaluated against benchmark and cost assumptions.”

Conditions:

- Must disclose universe, dates, benchmark, holding period, transaction cost assumptions, and whether results are gross or net.
- Must not imply real-world investability unless liquidity, turnover, slippage, and execution assumptions are included.
- Must not use cherry-picked examples as representative evidence.

Not allowed:

- “Proven to outperform”
- “Market-beating AI”
- “Users would have made X%”
- “Guaranteed edge”

### Level 2 — Paper-Trading / Shadow Portfolio Evidence

Status: live forward test or shadow portfolio has run for a defined period without real client capital.

Allowed claims:

- “The model is being forward-tested in a paper portfolio.”
- “Paper-trading results are monitored against benchmark, turnover, drawdown, and cost assumptions.”
- “Forward testing is designed to test whether backtested evidence survives live data conditions.”

Conditions:

- Must label paper results as paper results.
- Must state that paper trading does not reflect real execution, taxes, client behaviour, or full trading frictions.
- Must report losing periods as well as winning periods.

Not allowed:

- “Live proven alpha”
- “Real investor returns”
- “Verified performance” unless independently verified.

### Level 3 — Verified Live Evidence

Status: model has live results, independently verified or compliance-reviewed, with adequate track record.

Allowed claims may expand only after legal/compliance approval.

Possible future claims:

- “Verified historical performance over [period].”
- “Model portfolio performance shown net/gross of stated assumptions.”
- “Audited or independently reviewed methodology.”

Conditions:

- Performance methodology must be documented.
- Gross and net presentation must comply with applicable rules.
- Benchmark, fees, costs, risk, drawdown, and limitations must be shown clearly.
- No statement may imply future returns are guaranteed.

## 7. Required Product Disclaimers

### 7.1 Global Short Disclaimer

Use in footer, onboarding, and output views:

> Quantfore AI provides research support and evidence-backed monitoring. It is not financial advice and does not recommend that you buy, sell, or hold any security. Investing involves risk, including loss of capital.

### 7.2 Model Output Disclaimer

Use near model-generated thesis/risk views:

> This output is generated for research purposes only. It is based on available data, model assumptions, and monitored evidence. It may be incomplete or wrong and should not be treated as a personalised recommendation.

### 7.3 Backtest Disclaimer

Use on any backtest or strategy simulation:

> Backtested results are hypothetical and do not represent actual client returns. They may not reflect real-world execution, liquidity, slippage, fees, taxes, or behavioural constraints. Past performance and simulated performance do not guarantee future results.

### 7.4 Paper-Trading Disclaimer

Use on any paper portfolio:

> Paper-trading results are simulated and do not represent actual investment performance. They do not fully account for execution, taxes, investor behaviour, or all market frictions.

### 7.5 Data Disclaimer

Use in methodology or source panel:

> Quantfore AI relies on third-party and public data sources that may be delayed, incomplete, restated, or inaccurate. Model outputs should be reviewed alongside original source materials.

## 8. Output Language Policy

Quantfore AI should avoid direct instruction language.

### 8.1 Replace Advice-Like Language

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Buy | Positive research candidate |
| Sell | Thesis deterioration detected |
| Hold | Monitor / no material thesis change |
| Strong Buy | Attractive risk/reward screen |
| Strong Sell | Elevated downside / deteriorating evidence |
| You should add this | Consider for further research |
| You should exit | Review exposure and thesis assumptions |
| This will outperform | Model-implied risk/reward has improved |
| This is safe | Lower observed risk under current model assumptions |

### 8.2 Required Uncertainty Language

Every model-generated view must include at least one uncertainty marker:

- “based on available data”
- “under current model assumptions”
- “relative to sector peers”
- “over the selected time horizon”
- “subject to data quality and market regime risk”
- “requires human review before any investment action”

### 8.3 Confidence Labels

Allowed confidence labels:

- Low confidence
- Medium confidence
- High confidence

Each confidence label must be based on documented inputs, such as:

- signal agreement
- data freshness
- model historical error
- regime stability
- source quality
- missing data rate
- volatility/risk uncertainty

Do not use confidence labels as marketing hype.

## 9. Performance and Backtest Claims

### 9.1 General Rule

No performance claim may be shown publicly until it has passed the performance review checklist.

### 9.2 Performance Review Checklist

Before publishing any performance claim, document:

- model version
- universe
- benchmark
- date range
- rebalance frequency
- holding period
- gross and net returns
- transaction cost assumptions
- slippage assumptions
- liquidity constraints
- turnover
- drawdown
- volatility
- Sharpe / Sortino
- hit rate
- Rank IC or signal metric
- survivorship-bias controls
- point-in-time data controls
- corporate action handling
- out-of-sample period
- whether the result is backtested, paper-traded, or live
- reviewer and approval date

### 9.3 Banned Performance Practices

Do not:

- show only best-performing periods
- hide drawdowns
- omit costs
- compare gross model results against net benchmark results
- imply simulated returns were actual returns
- present isolated examples as representative
- use “would have made” language casually
- claim superiority without benchmark evidence
- publish model performance without versioning

- breakdowns of earnings-call changes
- risk/reward frameworks
- explanation of valuation, quality, momentum, revisions, and risk
- “how to think about” posts
- anonymised product workflows

Avoid:

- “Top 5 stocks to buy now”
- “AI says buy this”
- “This stock will explode”
- “Guaranteed upside”
- “Secret hedge fund picks”
- “Beat the market with our AI”
- “Copy this portfolio”

### 10.3 Safer Content Pattern

Use this pattern:

1. State the investment question.
2. Show the evidence that changed.
3. Show why the evidence matters.
4. State uncertainty and risks.
5. Avoid telling the reader what to do.

Example:

> “Company X’s thesis changed after margin guidance weakened and earnings revisions turned negative. Quantfore AI would flag this as thesis deterioration, not as an automatic sell signal. The right next step is to review whether the original investment case still holds.”

## 13. Evidence Thresholds for Stronger Claims

Quantfore AI may upgrade its claims only when evidence exists.

### 13.1 To Claim “Evidence-Backed”

Required:

- source citations in product output
- auditable data lineage
- model version ID
- explanation of signal components
- visible uncertainty markers

### 13.2 To Claim “Predictive Signal”

Required:

- point-in-time backtest
- out-of-sample validation
- signal metric such as Rank IC
- performance by regime and sector
- cost and turnover analysis
- robustness checks
- documented failure cases

### 13.3 To Claim “Improves Decision Quality”

Required:

- user research protocol
- pre/post task comparison
- decision-quality rubric
- statistically meaningful sample or clearly labelled qualitative study
- evidence that users understood risk better, avoided missed evidence, or made more consistent decisions

### 13.4 To Claim “Outperformance”

Required:

- legal/compliance approval
- independently reviewed or rigorously documented performance methodology
- benchmark comparison
- net-of-cost presentation
- drawdown and volatility disclosure
- relevant time period
- no cherry-picking
- prominent limitations

## 15. Red Flag Checklist

Before publishing any claim, ask:

1. Does this imply the product tells users what to buy or sell?
2. Does this imply guaranteed or likely profit?
3. Does this imply the model has been proven when it has not?
4. Does this hide risk while emphasising upside?
5. Does this present a backtest as actual performance?
6. Does this use cherry-picked examples?
7. Does this imply suitability for a specific user?
8. Does this sound like financial advice?
9. Does this depend on a claim we cannot evidence?
10. Would a regulator, customer, or journalist reasonably call this misleading?

If yes to any question, rewrite or escalate.

4. Do not publish backtests without methodology and limitations.

## 19. Practical Copy Rules

When in doubt, use this formula:

> Quantfore AI helps [user] monitor [evidence/change] so they can [research/review/compare] [investment thesis/risk/reward] more systematically.

Examples:

- “Quantfore AI helps investors monitor changes in company evidence so they can review investment theses more systematically.”
- “Quantfore AI helps users compare risk/reward across a watchlist using structured signals and source-backed explanations.”
- “Quantfore AI helps investors see what changed after earnings, guidance, valuation moves, and sentiment shifts.”

Avoid this formula:

> Quantfore AI tells [user] which [asset] to [buy/sell] so they can [make money/beat market/get rich].
